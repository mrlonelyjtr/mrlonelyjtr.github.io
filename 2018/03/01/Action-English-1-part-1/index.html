Action English 1 (part 1)

Posted on 2018-02-28 | In English

Ep 1

sound engineer 录音师

Rumor has it that…

it is said or it is generally considered that 人们都说…，据说…，据传…
(1) Rumor has it that Andy is a nice guy.
(2) Rumor has it that he is a rather difficult sound engineer.

matter

(not) matter to you 对你（不）重要
It doesn’t matter.
None of that matters.
(1) Your opinion really matters to me.
(2) You matter to me.
(3) It doesn’t matter if Mark isn’t coming. Maybe he just doesn’t do hip-hop you know, so let’s go.

counterfeit/genuine

假的/真的
counterfeit money/counterfeit watches
genuine leather/genuine watches
true love
real friends

(not) worth

…（不）值得
(1) Tibet is definitely worth seeing.
(2) This car isn’t worth the money you paid for it.
(3) Is he worth it?

Ep 2

cartoon 卡通片

Oh, come on!

委婉的表示你对一个人不信任或者不耐烦
(1) Oh, come on, you can do better than that.
come on
1.Hurry up! 你在催促一个人
(1) Come on, let’s get started.
2.加油
(1) Come on! Come on!

Who do we like for this?

凭我们以往的经验，会是谁干的呢？
(1) Who do you like for the soccer game tomorrow night? Brazil or Germany?
(2) Who do you like for the best actress at tomorrow’s Oscar’s?

Oh, gee…

表示事情没想到，惊讶
Gosh!
Oh my God!
Oh, no! 表示对事情非常抱怨，非常不满，发牢骚
Oh, oops! 表示不小心犯了个小错误

Go ahead.

可以，请便
(1) - Can I use the bathroom? - Go ahead.
(2) - Do you mind if I smoke here? - No, go ahead.

Ep 3

action movie 动作片

can’t get over

can’t forget sth 无法忘记…，无法从…的阴影中走出来
(1) The French soccer fans can’t get over the World Cup 2002.
(2) I just cannot get over Liz.
(3) I just can’t get over your decorator.

dwell on

驻扎，留守，引申为能不能不要再想…了，能不能别提及…抓住它不放手 stop thinking about
(1) We shouldn’t dwell on someone’s past.
(2) Hey, Kenny, it’s been five years since you lost the car. You shouldn’t dwell on this forever.

Get down!

1.crunch down a little bit 蹲下
2.face down, flat on the ground 趴下
Freeze 不许动 don’t move, stand still
Duck 把头低下去

What the hell is going on?

究竟出了什么事情？
What is going on? = What happened?/What’s wrong with this?
(1) What the hell are you talking about?
(2) Why the hell should I go there?

have the right to…

有权…
(1) I have the right to stay here as long as I want.
(2) You have the right to keep silent.

Ep 4

preview 预告片

play chicken

just the name of a dangerous game or just a test of courage, usually it’s played with cars
(1) When you drive you’d better not play chicken with bigger cars.

Don’t be a baby!

Don’t complain 不要发牢骚，不要抱怨
(1) Don’t be a baby. Go talk to your boss.
(2) He’s such a baby!
(3) Never, ever be a baby. You know?

keep my edge

stay sharp 让最棒的状态保持下去
lose my edge
(1) I have to speak English every day just in order to keep my edge.
(2) David Beckham is a great soccer player but if he doesn’t practise every day, he’ll lose his edge.

hone your skills

to practise to improve your skills 拼命地实践或者练习，为了提升你的技能
(1) Owning my own computer gives me a chance to hone my computer skills.
(2) Jane’s new job requires her to talk to lots of people. She’s really honing her people’s skills.

Ep 5

screen adaptation 改编

take your pick

you can choose any one you want 随你挑
(1) We have enough money to buy any DVD player this store has so take your pick.

line

在向异性套磁的时候说的话
That was a bad line. 看来刚才向你示爱的方式并不高明
(1) I didn’t give her my phone number because she used a terrible line.
(2) I’m not good at meeting girls because I don’t have any pick up lines.

ladies man

a man who’s good at attracting and talking to women 非常有女人缘的男性
(1) My cousin always has 2 or more women interested in him. He’s a real ladies man!
(2) My brother is a good looking guy and a smooth talker. Really he’s kind of a ladies man!

buy

believe 相信
She’s totally buying it. 她完全买账，完全相信对方
(1) He said he could help me get a job at his company but I don’t buy it. He doesn’t have that much influence.
(2) She told me she loved me but I don’t buy it. I saw her at dinner with her ex- last night.

Ep 6

log keeper 场记

It’s up to you.

It’s your decision. 由你来决定，由你说了算
(1) You can study English or Spanish. It’s up to you.
(2) You can have any sweater you want. It’s up to you. The color’s up to you.

What do you mean?

（懂一个人的话的表面意思，但是不明白为什么这么讲）你究竟是什么意思？
(1) What do you mean we have no more milk. I just bought some yesterday.

Can I get a minute?

hold on, just wait a minute 等以下好吗，能不能等我一下
(1) Gosh, I think I hurt myself. Can I get a minute?
(2) Hey, Michael, you walk too fast. I’m tired. Can I get a minute?
Just a minute. （打电话的时候）您稍等，我去给你找…

What’s the matter?

（表达对别人的关怀、问候）你怎么了？
What’s the problem? 出什么问题没有？
What’s got into you?
What’s going on?
(1) Why are you crying? What’s the matter?
(2) You are acting strangely. What’s the matter with you?

propose

just to ask someone to marry 求婚
propose to sb
(1) Did Ed propose to Jane, yet?
(2) How did you propose to your wife?
get engaged to 订婚
(1) A got engaged to B.
get married to 结婚
(1) A got married to B.

Ep 7

crew 剧组

bust my ass

1.make somebody’s life difficult or just give somebody trouble 让…的日子不好过，让…难堪
Stop busting my ass. 别再为难我了
(1) I wish the directors just would stop, you know, busting my ass because I already have lots of problems.
2.work hard 拼命地、努力地工作
(1) Every day I work, I bust my ass so my family can live well.

smooth

1.to be cool, to be skilled at 对…很擅长，很有一套
(1) He’s very smooth with women because he has dates every weekend.
2.just describe people, the kind of person who just can makes a difficult task look easy 形容一种人，这种人可以把很复杂的事情变得很简单的，一出手就有
(1) Somebody is a smooth.
3.be good at influencing people 指一种人，非常有感染力，非常有渲染力，可以很轻易得说服别人
(1) He has a very smooth manner, polite, interesting and a good talker.

sneak

move silently without being seen 走路的时候非常得小心，以便于不被人察觉，潜入
(1) If I come home late, I sneak into my house so I don’t wake anyone up.

head

go somewhere 去…地方（用将来时或现在进行时都可以）
(1) I’m so tired, I’m heading home.
(2) Are you heading to class later?
行为动词+home/here/there时，要把to给省略掉

memorize

to remember something 记住…，背下…
(1) I have to memorize forty vocabulary words today.

Ep 8

actor 演员

pass

1.give 给…
pass something to somebody/pass somebody something
(1) Could you pass me that paper?
(2) Joe is thirsty. Pass him a drink.
2.move in front of, go beyond 超越，超过…
(1) I was running as fast as I could, but that guy passed me.
3.succeed in doing 成功干成…，成功通过…
(1) I’m free! I’ve passed the final exams, finally.

kill somebody

win by a large margin, win by a big amount 狂胜…
(1) In the soccer game last week, Chinese killed Japanese.
(2) I love playing cards with Bob, because I kill him every time.

sharp

1.smart 形容一个人很聪明
2.clean, stylish, new 形容穿着比较精神和帅气
(1) That’s a really sharp looking suit.
(2) He is a sharp lawyer./I am a sharp student.
(3) That’s a sharp presentation.

eat Chinese

吃中国菜
eat American/Japanese
(1) Do you want to eat Italian or Japanese?
(2) I like to eat Indian.

Ep 9

editing 剪辑

the order of the day

something should be a priority …是头等大事，最重要的事情
(1) It’ll take three months to repair the house. Now patience is the order of the day for us.
(2) They won’t start shooting for a month. Now, learning your role is the order of the day.

beat sb. to the draw

someone has done something faster than someone else 抢先一步把事情做好
(1) We lost the project. Rainbow Company beat us to the draw.
(2) John beats Leon to the draw.

shut up

闭嘴
knock it off （语气比较强烈）
(1) Knock it off, would you? What are you trying to do? Jinx me?
be quiet 安静（礼貌一点）
(2) Could you be quiet for a while?

make yourself useful

do something useful, don’t waste time and don’t be lazy 使…变得有用起来
(1) Make yourself useful, ok? Go clean the garage.
(2) I visit my friend and she’s cooking dinner for me. So I ask her, “Well Leez, can I make myself useful?”

Ep 10

thriller 惊悚片

bluff

lie 撒谎，骗人，欺骗
(1) - Don’t hit me! OK? I know Kong Fu, I’m not bluffing. - You’re bluffing.
(2) - If you fire me, I’ll sue you. - You’re bluffing. Get out.

get something under control

把…在控制之内
(1) Everything is under control. 一切尽在掌握
(2) We’ve got the fire under control.
(3) The doctor told me she had the disease under control.
get something out of control …失控了
(1) We finally called the cops when things got out of control.
(2) Gosh! My weight is out of control.

any idea…

do you know… 你知不知道…
(1) Excuse me, any idea what time it is?
(2) Any idea when the mall will be closed?
(3) Any idea why Lara is not here?

Ep 11

close-up 特写

Ready to roll?

Are you ready to go? 你准备好了吗？我们可以启程了吗？我们可以开始干…了吗？
(1) I got every suitcase. Ready to roll?
(2) Thank God, everybody is on the set on time. Ready to roll?

believe it or not

你爱信不信
(1) I’m an agent as well, believe it or not.
(2) I’ve given up smoking, believe it or not.
(3) That guy wearing the brown suit is our teacher, believe it or not.

found out

发现…，知道…内幕
(1) I just found out I won the lottery.
(2) I finally found out who took my walkman.

I don’t get it.

I don’t understand.
get 理解，搞懂
(1) Got it? 你懂没懂？/I got it. 我懂了
(2) Sorry, I don’t get it. Could you please repeat it again?
(3) Well, that guy has only been here a month and he’s been promoted. I don’t get it.

Ep 12

studio 制片公司

Hold on!

Wait! 等一下，稍等一下
(1) - May I speak with Joe? - Hold on please. Joe is on another line.
(2) Hold on! Hold on! Could you move a little bit close?

all in all

总之，说一千道一万（在对一个事物进行评价，最后要有一个收尾，要下一个定性的结论）
(1) All in all, even though he has problems, he is a good soccer player.
(2) All in all, Action English is a very good English program.

except

除了…之外
1.+for+词/短语
2.+句子
(1) She’s a very nice girl except she is a little bit selfish.

tease

not be serious 跟…的关系，对…的态度不是认真的，只是玩玩儿而已
(1) She’s always talking to men but she never gives them her phone number. She’s just teasing them.

must have done

必…（表示一种主观推测）
(1) You must have been hungry.
(2) Leave him alone. He must have been drunk.

Ep 13

western 西部片

straight up

absolutely, correct 完全正确，你说的完全对（用于非正式的场合）
absolutely/exactly/right/correct/You said it./You can say that again. （在很正式的场合表示你完全同意对方的意见）

gorgeous

very very beautiful 形容一个美好的事物或人
pretty < beautiful < gorgeous
(1) She’s a gorgeous woman.
cool
(1) That girl is so cool.
(2) This car/Your bag is so cool.
killer
(1) She has killer legs.
(2) He is a lady killer.
hot
(1) He or she is so hot.
personality （表示一个人很有个性）
(1) He has a strong personality.
nice
(1) She’s a nice girl.
(2) I’m a nice guy.
(3) Nice car/shoes!

You could do better.

你可以做得更好（一般只形容人或工作，很委婉，很技巧）

feed

give sb. food 喂，引申为助长
(1) feed her perfectionism
(2) You have to feed your dreams.

Ep 14

movie camera 电影摄影机

Can I get a lift?

Can you let me ride in your car? 我能搭下你的车吗？
(1) I can give you a lift.

Better luck next time.

下次祝你走运
Good luck. 祝你走运
Just my luck./Bad luck. 我可真够背的

make it up to sb.

对人进行弥补
(1) I don’t know what I can do to make it up to you.
make up for sth. 对事情进行弥补
make up with sb. 与…重归于好
(1) Jenny and I made up with each other and are still very good friends.
makeup n.化妆品
make up v.化妆
(1) She is making up.

Ep 15

bootleg 盗版

whacked out

completely insane, very very crazy 精神完全失常了，完全疯掉了
(1) That girl is whacked out.
(2) Don’t get whacked out!/Don’t be crazy!
chill 镇定，控制住情绪

I had a hunch on that.

我对…有预感
(1) I have a hunch on that I’ll never see the money again.
(2) - Hey man, why did you bet all the money on the horse? - I had a hunch on that.

- You got that? - Yes, I do got it.

-Do you understand? -Yes, understand totally. -你懂没懂？-我都懂了

turn over a new leaf

to get a new start, to change everything about yourself 把过去的恶习统统都抛弃，重新开始做人，重新开始
(1) I used to be really lazy. My boss fired me. Well, I work hard from now on. I’m turning over a new leaf.
(2) I’m turning over a new leaf. No more drinking, no more smoking. It’s a healthy life from now on.

I’m at a loss for words.

我真不知该说什么，我都说不出话来了，我哑口无言了
(1) I’m at a loss for words. How dare you accuse me of stealing!
(2) This is the most wonderful gift I’ve ever received. I’m at a loss for words.
(3) Gosh, that girl is dead gorgeous. I’m at a loss for words.

Ep 16

science fiction 科幻片

tell somebody on somebody

告…的状
(1) Give back my toy, or I will tell Dad on you.
(2) I broke the vase, please don’t tell on me.
(3) I lost the computer disc. Please don’t tell the boss on me.

an idiot

a very very stupid person 傻瓜，白痴，蠢货
idiotic a.
(1) Mary is quitting university to become a singer. She’s not an idiot but this is the most idiotic plan I have ever heard.
(2) Don’t listen to what he says. He’s an idiot.

I’m a little edgy.

1.to be nervous 有点儿紧张
2.to be impatient or angry 有点儿不耐烦，情绪不太好
(1) He’s got a job interview tomorrow. He’s a little edgy right now.
(2) Erin hasn’t slept for two days. So stay away from her, she’s a little edgy.
(3) Anyway I’m not edgy at all, so don’t stay away from me.

Ep 17

sound track 原声碟

a head start

an early start 走在别人的前面
(1) You should get up early and study. Get a head start on your classmates.
(2) Craig is a slow runner, but he got a head start on the others, so he might win the race.

I had no idea.

I didn’t know. (But it’s very strong.) 我不知道，我怎么会知道呢（语气更加强烈）
(1) Duran can play the violin. I had no idea he was so talented!
(2) Don’t ask me where your car keys are. I have no idea!

mad at

angry 生…的气
be mad about 对…很入迷
(1) You’d better go talk to Frank. He’s really mad at you.
(2) If we’re lost, don’t get mad at me! You’re the one who forgot the map!

What’s up?

1.How are you? 打招呼（和熟人之间）
2.What’s the matter with you?/What’s wrong with you?/What’s the matter? 有什么事情吗？出什么事情了吗？
(1) Hey, what’s up?
(2) You look really angry, what’s up?
